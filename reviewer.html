The review problem

A final file shows too little. A long AI chat shows too much.

A short record gives the reviewer a readable starting point.

The reviewer asks the maker to create the record. The reviewer does not create it for someone else.

What to ask for

Ask the maker for a Human Contribution Record.

The record is based on materials provided by the person who made the work. It helps review the process, but it is not independent proof.

The maker provides

  • final work
  • AI chats or prompts
  • drafts, notes, and screenshots
  • versions, source files, or file history

The reviewer sees

  • what the person did
  • how AI helped
  • what materials exist
  • what questions remain open

How it works

A clearer review process.

1

Reviewer asks for a record

Ask for process context instead of judging only from the final file.

2

Maker creates the record

The person who made the work provides the relevant materials.

3

Reviewer reads the summary

See the main human decisions, AI help, materials, and stated limits.

4

Reviewer follows up

Ask for more details or materials when the summary leaves questions open.
