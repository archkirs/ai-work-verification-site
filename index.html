The problem

A final file shows the result, not the process.

A long AI chat is difficult to review. Drafts and notes may be spread across several tools.

A short record brings the main information together without pretending to make the final decision.

What goes in

You provide the work and the process materials.

The record is based on materials provided by the person who made the work. It helps review the process, but it is not independent proof.

You provide

  • final work
  • AI chats or prompts
  • drafts and notes
  • versions, screenshots, or file history

The record shows

  • what you did
  • how AI helped
  • what materials exist
  • what still needs review

How it works

Four simple steps.

1

Start with the work

Use the final assignment, image, text, design, research, or other work.

2

Add process materials

Add the drafts, notes, AI chats, versions, and files that already exist.

3

Create the record

The main human decisions, AI help, materials, and limits are summarized.

4

Share it

A reviewer reads a short process summary and asks follow-up questions if needed.

Use cases

Limits

The record helps review. It does not make the decision.
